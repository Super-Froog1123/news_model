3. 纯文字模板（初始设置p底部外边距30px）

“Da Chang”, a Chinese word meaning “big factory”, usually refers to China’s most well-known tech giants, including ByteDance, Alibaba, Tencent, Pinduoduo, NetEase, etc.

You might not be familiar with these companies, but you must have heard about Tik Tok, Taobao, and WeChat, the applications used by billions of people worldwide. These internet giants have profoundly reshaped the Chinese people’s everyday lives, from online shopping to entertainment, from daily communication to mobile payment.

In past decades, the dramatically increasing net worth of these China’s internet companies has made them the most highly-valued companies in the world.

It seems exciting to be part of a world-famous big tech that influences people’s daily lives. But discussion about whether it is an excellent choice to work in Da Chang has become controversial in recent years.

Despite the popularity of Da Chang working opportunities, some said their work in these companies fails to meet their expectations. Joy Su, a former intern of Tencent Beijing, said the long working hours and repetitive work in Da Chang disappointed her. “I feel like I am a tiny piece of wood in a huge fireplace. Everyday I keep this fire burning with my past experience and knowledge, but I’m not learning anything new. If I stay in Da Chang, I know sooner or later I will be used up.” said Joy Su.

7. 横向双图片模板（照片会超出文档一点）

Though competitive, Da Chang provides a relatively high wage for interns. “My intern salary at ByteDance was RMB 150 per day, plus RMB 1500 housing allowance every month, and the company also provides free breakfast and dinner,” said Sophia Sheng.

Ray, a full-time programmer from Tencent Xi'an, told us that interns in her team usually earn RMB 200 to 300 per day.

8. 横向图表文字模板（左右可以替换）

Data from the 2020 Chinese university graduate employability report shows that remuneration is the biggest concern for over 60% of the survey participants.

According to a survey involving more than 700 respondents, the median intern’s salary at internet companies is between RMB 2,000 to 3,000 per month. The report also shows that most interns from these internet companies can obtain above-average pay, which is attractive to fresh graduates.
